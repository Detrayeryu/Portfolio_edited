School

In Australia, I attended a school called Melba Copland Secondary School. There, I was part of a program called the "magellan program". The magellan program is a program where Year 10 students like myself, go to the college and take college subjects with the other Year 11 and 12 students. Over there, I took several different subjects including English and literature, Y11 Specialist Math, Physics, Chemistry, Digital Technologies, Design and Emerging Technologies, PE and a Year 10 special subject called "Personal Projects".

Back in Singapore, I was in Bedok View Secondary School. There, I studied through Secondary 1 to Secondary 3. The subjects i took include Emaths, Amaths, English, Pure Physics, Pure Chemistry, Combined Geography, Social studies and Chinese.

I would say that my strongest subject is maths. I have always been good at maths and even in Australia, I decided to take a math subject 1 grade above mine just so I dont fall short behind all my friends at math in Singapore.

My Journey

I was first introduced to Coding as a child when I was in Primary 6. I was introduced to the software known as "scratch". I was fascinated by how you could make things move and react by "inputing" some "code". I later found out that scratch was just a simplified version of what coding really is. When I reached Secondary 1, I did more research into what coding is and raised my interest to dad. My father was supportive as I didnt really have any interest besides gaming. My father than sent me on a C-sharp course to see let me experience it myself. During the course, i learnt the basics of coding but there were some parts i wasn't quite sure yet. At the end of the course i found out just how tiring and difficult coding really is. I took a break from coding for half a year. When i was in Secondary 2, My father enrolled me into SGCodeCampus to learn python. I understood the course well and contiunue to take classes all the way to the end of 2019. In the 2 years, I have been taught Python and HTML as well as CSS. In the year 2020, I became more active in coding. I had to stop my classes as I was in Australia. There, I started doing free online courses on websites like FreeCodeCamp, Codecademy, HackerRank and Singtel's Life Joureny Cyber security course. I also started working on a discord bot for my "Personal Projects". I took part in several coding events including YCEP 2020 and CyberTaipan.
